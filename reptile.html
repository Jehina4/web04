Phelsuma guimbeaui

Day Geckos as you would imagine from their name are mainly active during the day in contrast with most other species of Gecko. Most Day Geckos are brightly coloured with vivid greens, blues, reds and oranges. An exception to this in Mauritius is Gunther’s Gecko (Phelsuma guentheri), now restricted to Round Island, which is greyish or greyish-brown. Day Geckos have distinctive toe pads that allow them to walk on vertical or overhanging surfaces. Unsurprisingly Day Geckos spend a lot of time in trees. The remaining mainland species, all of which are much smaller than Gunther’s Gecko like to shelter in tree cavities and in tightly packed clusters of leaves of the type found in native Palm species and Screwpines (Vacoas species) and in the introduced Traveller’s palm (Ravenala madagascariensis). Day Geckos feed on insects and other invertebrates and also nectar, pollen and fruits.

Phelsuma cepediana

Blue-tailed day geckos (Phelsuma cepediana) are a diurnal, omnivorous type of arboreal gecko primarily native to Mauritius. They prefer tropical forest for habitat with plenty of rainfall, and as a strictly arboreal species, they spend most of their lives up off the ground. Blue-tailed day geckos generally grow 3.75-5.5″ / 9.5-14cm long, and are among the most brightly-colored day geckos. They have a bright green to blue base color, with a dark stripe on each side from the tip of the snout to the base of the tail, rust- to red-colored spots on the back, and darker limbs. Males are generally more colorful than females. Their bodies have a sharply tapered snout, round lidless eyes, sticky toe pads, a moderate-length tail, and delicate velvety skin. Blue-tailed day geckos are particularly sensitive to improper husbandry, which makes them more difficult to care for and breed. Captive-bred individuals are likely to do the best as pets. When appropriate blue-tailed day gecko care is provided, you may expect of lifespan of up to 15+ years.

Phelsuma ornata

helsuma ornata commonly known as the Mauritius ornate day gecko, is as its name suggests a diurnal species of geckos and one of the smallest day geckos reaching a total length of 12 centimeters, with males slightly larger than females. Like other day geckos, they are characterized by vivid body colours. Their backs can be bluish green, green with a blue area on the forward part of its back or completely blue covered with red dots. The back of its neck and its head is greyish brown. The snout of this gecko consists of an intricate pattern of cyan, white, red and dark blue while their turquoise tail bears red transversal stripes. The species are found on the island Mauritius and some surrounding islets where it inhabits different trees and bushes and feeds on insects and nectar.

Leiolopisma telfairii

The Telfair's skink (Leiolopisma telfairii), also known as the Round Island skink, is another critically important endemic reptile of Mauritius. Its story is a classic example of conservation success against overwhelming odds.elfair's skinks are relatively large for a skink, reaching a total length (including tail) of 22-27 cm, with some sources stating up to 30-40 cm. They have a brownish-grey body often mottled with dark brown spots, and their small scales can exhibit an iridescence in sunlight. They have a cylindrical body and short, vigorous legs. They can also drop their tails as a defense mechanism, which later regenerates.They prefer vegetated palm forest and weed-based habitats, hiding among leaf litter and rocks during the hottest parts of the day. They are largely terrestrial but can be found climbing.

Gongylomorphus fontenayi

Gongylomorphus fontenayi, often referred to as the Orange-tailed Skink or Macchabé Skink, is an endemic species of skink found in Mauritius. Its taxonomic status has seen some changes, with it sometimes being considered a subspecies of Gongylomorphus bojerii (as Gongylomorphus bojerii fontenayi), but recent genetic analyses often elevate it to full species status.t's a relatively small skink. Adult Macchabé Skinks typically have a snout-vent length (SVL) of around 44-48 mm. The "Orange-tailed Skink" form, particularly the one from Flat Island, is larger, with males reaching an SVL of around 68 mm and females around 50 mm.The Orange-tailed Skink is typically brown with an orange tinge, a bright orange ventral (belly) surface, and an orange/red tail. The Macchabé Skink is described as being chocolate-colored.

Cylindraspis triserrata – Domed Mauritius Giant Tortoise

The Cylindraspis triserrata, commonly known as the Domed Mauritius Giant Tortoise, was one of two species of giant tortoises native to Mauritius. It played a vital role in shaping the island's ecosystem. This species had a high, rounded shell that helped protect it from predators and environmental damage. It grazed on grasses, fallen fruits, and low shrubs.Found across the main island of Mauritius and possibly nearby islets.Preferred flat and forested regions. It got extinct due to sailors and settlers captured and killed them for food.

Cylindraspis inepta – Saddle-backed Mauritius Giant Tortoise

The Cylindraspis inepta was the second of the two giant tortoise species that once roamed Mauritius. It was unique for its saddle-backed shell, which allowed it to reach higher vegetation compared to the domed species.
Inhabited Mauritius, especially drier regions and lowlands.
It got extinct due to mass hunting by sailors for meat during the 1600s–1700s. and habitat loss due to deforestation.
